A little about myself

Born in Scotland, I am a computer science student at the University of Waikato, New Zealand.

I started programming back in 2006 with BASIC and later scripting in Bethesda's Oblivion.

In 2011, I started using the Cryengine SDK after playing Crysis 2 and loving the fidelity of it. I also ended up modding in Skyrim.

I learned Python, HTML5 and CSS3 in 2012 at Cambridge High School. And continued mod games through my High School years but didn't really make anything noteworthy

I was fortunate to join a startup in 2014 and attain one of ten computer science scholarships to Waikato University. During my time at Nyriad, I learned C++ and CUDA. I also learned how to peer program and work in an agile development environment.

As a game project for a paper at Waikato, I took a role of artist due to my want to learn art. I also setup and managed the Perforce source control server. About half way through our project, the team was able to take a trip up to the Auckland University of Technology to do motion capture where I worked with MotionBuilder.

Since late 2015, I have been learning computer graphics. I managed a basic Direct3D11 implementation in early december and a basic Vulkan implementation in late February 2016. Since then I have toyed with raytracer implementations and have done a more recent Vulkan implementation for a paper at Waikato.

In the latter half of 2016, I tutored Game Design at Waikato and attained Citizenship of New Zealand.
