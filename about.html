A little about myself

Hey, I'm Chris. I am a computer science student at the University of Waikato in New Zealand and I hail from Scotland. I emigrated to New Zealand in August 2011 and almost have citizenship.

I started programming back in 2009 with BASIC and later scripting in Oblivion. I remember loving the openness of creating programs however simple they were back then.

In 2011, I started using the Cryengine SDK after playing Crysis 2 and loving the fidelity of it. I also ended up modding in Skyrim and learning how the basics of object orientation worked even though I didn't know what it was at the time.

I learned Python, HTML5 and CSS3 in 2012 at Cambridge High School. I was also taught far more about programming concepts that I missed by self teaching.

I was fortunate to join a startup in 2014 and attain one of ten scholarships to Waikato University. During my time at Nyriad, I learned C++ and CUDA. I also learned how to peer program and work in an agile development environment.

As a project for a paper at Waikato, I took a role as both an artist and a programmer due to us deciding to make a game for our project and my want to learn art. I also setup and managed the Perforce source control server. About half way through our project, the team was able to take a trip up to the Auckland University of Technology to do motion capture.

Since late 2015, I have been learning computer graphics. I managed a basic Direct3D11 implementation in early december and a basic Vulkan implementation in late February 2016.

I have also taken an interest in the artificial intelligence field and have started creating neural networks in Tensorflow.
